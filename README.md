# **Olympics Data Analysis (Python)**

## **Description**
This project involves comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on historical Olympics data (1896â€“2024), focusing on participation trends, medal distribution across countries and sports, and gender analysis over time.

## **Dataset**
* **Source:** `olympics_dataset.csv`
* **Size:** 252,565 records across 11 columns.
* **Key Columns:** `Name`, `Sex`, `Team`, `NOC`, `Year`, `Season`, `City`, `Sport`, `Event`, and `Medal`.

## **Technologies Used**
* **Python**
* **Pandas** for data loading, cleaning, and manipulation.
* **NumPy** for numerical operations.
* **Matplotlib** for visualization (Bar charts, Line plots, Heatmaps).

## **Key Findings & Visualizations**

### **Overall Statistics**
* **Total Records (Events):** 252,565
* **Total Medal Records:** 38,818
* **Unique Teams/Countries:** 1,193
* **Unique Sports:** 76

### **Medal Distribution & Trends**
* **Medal Count by Type:** Bronze (13,070), Gold (13,002), Silver (12,746).
* **Top Medaling Country:** United States (5,305 medals).
* **Top Medaling Sport:** Athletics (4,429 medals).
* **Top Host Cities by Medals:** Paris, London, and Tokyo.
* **Temporal Trend:** Total medals awarded have generally trended upwards from 1896 to 2024.

### **Gender Analysis**
* **Overall Participation:** Male participants (178,544) significantly outnumber Female participants (74,021).
* **Medal Share:** Male athletes account for approximately 70% of total medals, while female athletes account for approximately 30%.
* **Trend:** Female participation has shown a significant and continuous increase over the decades.
